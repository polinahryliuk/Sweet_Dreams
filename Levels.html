Find out your level of culinary skills

Determining your culinary level involves assessing your skills, knowledge, and experience in the kitchen. Here's a general guide to help you identify your culinary level:

1.Beginner:

Limited experience in the kitchen. Basic knowledge of cooking techniques and terminology. Comfortable with simple recipes and tasks such as boiling, frying, and baking. Still learning fundamental skills like knife handling, seasoning, and recipe following.

2.Intermediate:

Moderate experience and confidence in the kitchen. Familiar with a wider range of cooking techniques and ingredients. Able to follow moderately complex recipes and adapt them to personal taste. Developing skills in flavor balancing, sauce making, and recipe improvisation.

3.Advanced:

Extensive experience and proficiency in the kitchen. Comfortable with a variety of advanced cooking techniques and methods. Able to create complex dishes from scratch and experiment with flavors and textures. Knowledgeable about different cuisines, food pairings, and culinary trends. Capable of executing multi-course meals and catering to specific dietary preferences or restrictions.
